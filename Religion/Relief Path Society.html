Overview

Official name

Relief Path Society (解脱道会, Jiětuō Dào Huì)
Public branding: Relief Path or The Path of Relief
Internal name among the Delivered: The Gentle Exit or simply The Exit

Sometimes members just call it “the Dao” (道) in casual conversation, which sounds completely innocuous to outsiders.

  • Motto (public): “You do not owe the world another day.”
  • Motto (internal): “The kindest hand is the one that finally lets go.”
  • Founding: Publicly 5017; Actually: Late 5018, six months after Gao Cong was permanently banished from The Last Order (TLO) for sexual predation during unsanctioned “admission ceremonies.”
  • Founder: “The First Friend” – Gao Cong (高聪), self-styled “Empty Guide”
  • Legal status: Registered in Chinnan as a non-profit religious society and federally as a charity (status never revoked because all financial records are impeccable and donations are recorded as “end-of-life planning gifts”).

Doctrine

Core dogma (what they tell new recruits)

“Life is an endless burden of work, desire, and responsibility.
The world was not meant to continue forever.
True compassion is to help yourself and others lay down the burden gently, without pain, without guilt.
There is a quiet place beyond effort where everything is already forgiven.
We do not force anyone. We only offer relief when you are ready.”

It is deliberately framed as a soft, almost Buddhist/Taoist-sounding philosophy of “letting go,” anti-natalism lite, and extreme work-life-balance escapism. No one hears the words “death,” “sterilization,” or “slavery” in the first two years.

Public doctrine

A modern lay movement that teaches “burdenless living.” Core texts:

  • 《放下书》 The Book of Laying Down (looks like minimalist poetry)
  • 《无债一身轻》 A Life Without Debt (interpreted by outsiders as financial advice)

Real doctrine (revealed only after Level 4)

All suffering comes from the refusal to stop.
Reproduction is violence.
Continued existence after one’s “useful beauty” has faded is cruelty to the self.
The highest mercy is to deliver others — willingly or not — to Permanent Relief.

Revealed only after years of love-bombing and isolation:

  1. Relief is only complete when you stop reproducing and stop prolonging life. Procreation = the ultimate selfishness. Continuing to exist when you are suffering = attaching to illusion.
  2. The highest act of compassion is to help others reach Permanent Relief (suicide, euthanasia, or, for valuable recruits, being sold to Interstellar Operations as a “final donation of the body”).
  3. There are three legitimate ways to leave the burden behind:
    • Natural Exit (refuse medical care, slow fade)
    • Assisted Exit (cult provides pills, helium, or “quiet ceremonies”)
    • Donated Exit (the prettiest or healthiest members are sold off-planet; the money funds the cult, and the member “escapes the world entirely”)

Events

Public activities (the bait)

  • Free “stress-relief workshops” and “burden-sharing circles” (group therapy vibes)
  • Communal dinners, board-game nights, house-cleaning parties for overwhelmed single mothers or burnt-out office workers
  • “Digital detox retreats” in the countryside
  • Volunteer groups that help elderly people “simplify their lives” (i.e., convince them to sign over property or stop medical treatment)
  • A very aesthetic, calm Instagram/TikTok presence with pastel colors, lo-fi music, and quotes like
    “It’s okay to stop striving.”
    “You don’t owe the future anything.”

Everything looks like the most wholesome mindfulness community you have ever seen.

Symbols

  • A white cup turned upside-down on a pale blue circle
  • Soft chime sound (three descending notes) played at the start of every gathering
  • Members wear a thin silver bracelet engraved with “Already Free”

The Relief Path Society is, from the outside, the friendliest, calmest, most ethical community you could ever stumble into.
From the inside, it is a perfectly oiled conveyor belt that turns loneliness into property, bodies into cash, and hope into a quiet, pastel-colored grave.

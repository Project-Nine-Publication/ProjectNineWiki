Profile

Early Life and Education

Li Wenhao was born in the coastal city of Qingdao, Shandong Province, during the tail end of the Cultural Revolution. Raised in a household of educators—his father a physics teacher and his mother a librarian—Li showed an early fascination with logic puzzles and foreign technology magazines smuggled through seafarers' trade routes.

He enrolled in Tsinghua University in 1985, where he earned his B.Sc. in Computer Science and later completed his Ph.D. in Natural Language Processing (NLP) in 1993. During his postgraduate years, Li was a visiting scholar at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, where he was exposed to early web protocols and academic mailing lists. This exposure became pivotal in shaping his later ideas around information indexing and global knowledge accessibility.

Career

Founding of Haoyang (1994)

While lecturing at Tsinghua, Li collaborated with Chen Yujing, a former investigative journalist and fellow advocate for open knowledge. The two launched HY Bulletin (浩阳布告栏), an internal digital board system for student discussions and class notes, inspired by Usenet and early BBS systems.

In 1997, the project spun off as a private company — Haoyang Technologies, Inc. — with Li as Chief Architect and Chairman. His design for the Haoyang Index emphasized not just searchability, but emotional and contextual understanding, blending manual curation with algorithmic recommendations — a hybrid approach that gained early popularity in Asia.

Digital Spring and Soft Globalism

From 1999 to 2009, Li oversaw Haoyang’s rise during the “Digital Spring,” advocating for what he called “Soft Globalism” — a strategy of respectful, non-disruptive expansion into developing markets through language localization, ethical data practices, and emotional UX.

He was instrumental in establishing the Brand Harmony Index (a user-facing quality score for content and ads) and pushed for cross-cultural training programs in Haoyang's R&D centers, particularly in Nairobi and Jakarta.

The Qiming Era and Ethical AI (2015–present)

With the launch of Haoyang’s AI division in 2015, Li transitioned from CEO to Chief Visionary Officer (CVO). He led the creation of Qiming (启明) — a sentiment-aware AI platform — and introduced internal AI ethics charters still referenced in global policy discussions.

Following criticism during the “Sunlight Manipulation” scandal in 2024, Li appeared publicly for the first time in years to address transparency concerns. His speech at the Everything Good Summit 2025, titled “Algorithmic Kindness,” is widely credited with reshaping public discourse on digital mood engineering.

Influence and Legacy

Li Wenhao is frequently cited as one of the founding figures of the East Asian Digital Renaissance, alongside figures like Masato Kuroda (Wakabayashi Index) and Nawal Sharif (PanAfricaNet). His model of “Emotionally Intelligent Computing” has been incorporated into university curricula and global tech ethics frameworks.

He is known for rejecting the “move fast and break things” ethos, advocating instead for slow, harmonious growth, and interface optimism — the belief that technology interfaces can psychologically uplift users.

Personal Life

A known introvert, Li is rarely seen in public outside Haoyang summits and academic panels. He is an avid Go player, publishes essays under the pseudonym "SunBehindClouds" (云后日), and maintains a personal archive of calligraphy from Song dynasty scholars.

Li lives in a minimalist compound in Chengdu designed by Japanese architect Morihiro Senda and rarely travels outside Asia.

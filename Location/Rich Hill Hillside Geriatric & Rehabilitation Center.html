City: Rich Hill, in the municipality of Moana Planet: Tellus Type: Hospital, Assisted Living Complex

Rich Hill Hillside Geriatric & Rehabilitation Center (often referred to as Hillside Geriatric or HGRC) is a specialized healthcare facility providing short-term rehabilitation, long-term geriatric care, and palliative services to residents of Rich Hill, a town of approximately 250,000. The Center is situated on the western edge of Rich Hill, adjacent to a historic retirement community and assisted living complex once known as the Rich Hill Tuberculosis Sanatorium. Notably, a portion of the Center’s underground tunnels originated as a former military installation, which has since been adapted for emergency shelter against periodic Arcane Storms — a magical climate phenomenon unique to the region.

Overview

  • Type: Geriatric & Rehabilitation Hospital
  • Location: West Rich Hill, near foothills and low mountains
  • Beds: ~100 (split between rehabilitation, geriatric care, and palliative services)
  • Affiliated with: Rich Hill Medical Center (RHMC), via a subterranean corridor
  • Established: Mid-50th century (converted from a late 49th-century sanatorium)
  • Known for: Underground bunker system, holistic therapy programs, specialized senior care

History

Early Sanatorium Era

The hillside complex traces its origins to the year of 4895, when local philanthropists funded the Rich Hill Tuberculosis Sanatorium, capitalizing on the area’s elevated terrain and purportedly “therapeutic” air currents. During the early 50th century, the site expanded to include multiple wards, pavilions, and convalescent gardens, catering to hundreds of patients seeking long-term TB treatment.

With the decline in tuberculosis prevalence and the development of effective antibiotic treatments in the mid-50th century, much of the sanatorium’s infrastructure became underutilized. Local authorities considered demolishing the site but ultimately chose to convert it into a geriatric facility, given Rich Hill’s growing senior population.

Cold War Bunker and Tunnel Network

From 4955 onward, a portion of the sanatorium’s outer grounds was quietly repurposed for military defensive infrastructure during the continent’s Cold War era. Engineers constructed reinforced bunkers and a network of tunnels under the hillside, intended to shelter key personnel and medical staff in the event of armed conflict or magical calamities.

Following decommission in the late 4970s, these bunkers remained in partial use for supply storage until the facility’s transition into a modern geriatric center. The subterranean corridors — several of which extend beyond the main building — were subsequently renovated to provide emergency shelter and connect HGRC with the larger Rich Hill Medical Center (RHMC), located approximately 2 km away.

Conversion to a Modern Geriatric & Rehabilitation Center

By 4990, the site was officially renamed the Rich Hill Hillside Geriatric & Rehabilitation Center. Upgrades included converting old sanatorium wards into patient-friendly suites, installing modern therapy facilities, and creating specialized units for dementia care. The bunker system was also adapted for public safety, particularly for Arcane Storms, a magical weather phenomenon that occasionally impacts the region.

Arcane Storm Phenomenon

Arcane Storms are powerful, sporadic weather events tied to the planet’s unique magical climate. Characterized by:

  • Intense flashes of luminescent energy in the sky, accompanied by atmospheric distortions.
  • Rapid changes in atmospheric pressure that can cause severe health risks for vulnerable populations, including older adults and post-surgery patients.
  • Localized disruptions in magical fields, potentially interfering with modern medical equipment if not properly shielded.

The Hillside Geriatric campus uses its underground shelters for patient relocation during severe Arcane Storms, ensuring continuity of care and protection from fluctuating magical energies.

Campus and Facilities

1. Main Geriatric Tower

  • Floors: 4 above ground, 1 subterranean level
  • Services:
    • Long-Term Care Units: Focused on seniors with chronic conditions requiring ongoing monitoring.
    • Palliative Care Wing: Accommodates terminally ill patients and hospice services.
    • Recreational Spaces: Activity rooms, a greenhouse garden on the rooftop, and communal lounges.

2. Rehabilitation Pavilion

  • Floors: 2 above ground
  • Services:
    • Physical Therapy Suites: Equipped with modern equipment, therapy pools, and an indoor walking track.
    • Occupational Therapy Rooms: Fitted with adaptive living setups, training kitchens, and skill practice areas.
    • Wellness Programs: Group exercise classes, yoga adapted for seniors, and post-operative therapy sessions.

3. Historic Sanatorium Wing

  • Floors: 3, partially renovated
  • Notable Features:
    • Original Victorian Architecture: Retained in hallways and exterior facades for heritage preservation.
    • Heritage Exhibits: Small displays illustrating the building’s past as a TB sanatorium.
    • Administrative Offices: Some spaces converted for hospital administration, staff training, and volunteer coordination.

4. Underground Bunker & Tunnel Network

  • Extent: Spans beneath the hillside, connecting storage chambers, sheltered patient rooms, and an emergency operations center.
  • Entrances:
    • Primary Access: Through the lower level of the Main Geriatric Tower.
    • Secondary Tunnels: One corridor leads toward Rich Hill Medical Center (RHMC); additional locked entrances are located near the mountain foot for deliveries and staff emergencies.
  • Emergency Shelter Facilities:
    • Arcane Storm Safe Rooms: Shielded zones with reinforced walls and medical stations.
    • Long-Term Provisions Storage: Capable of housing staff and patients for multiple days if needed.
    • Disaster Preparedness Equipment: Generators, oxygen supplies, and magical dampeners (to mitigate interference with devices).

5. Assisted Living Courtyard & Community

  • Location: Adjacent to the main center, formerly the Sanatorium gardens.
  • Services: Independent or semi-independent living suites for seniors who need minimal nursing care.
  • Facilities:
    • Community Center: Hosts social events, educational workshops, and group meals.
    • Outdoor Walkways: Landscaped paths with benches and exercise stations, designed to be safe for those with mobility aids.

Programs and Patient Care

  1. Geriatric Medicine & Dementia Care: Board-certified geriatricians, specialized nurses, and an on-site memory care unit.
  2. Transitional Rehabilitation: Short-term stays post-surgery or hospitalization, with a focus on returning patients to independent living.
  3. Holistic Therapy: Partnerships with local gyms and senior centers, offering gentle aerobics, aqua therapy, and relaxation techniques for older adults.
  4. Emergency Preparedness Drills: Regular training to prepare staff and patients for Arcane Storms, including swift relocation to underground bunkers.

Governance and Funding

  • Ownership: Operated by a nonprofit healthcare trust in partnership with Rich Hill’s municipal government and private donors.
  • Funding Sources:
    • Local government health grants
    • Contributions from philanthropic organizations
    • Patient insurance and private pay
    • Estate donations from Rich Hill’s senior community
  • Regulatory Oversight: Subject to Rich Hill’s Department of Health and, in the case of specialized magical storm protocols, the National Arcane Safety Board (NASB).

Cultural Significance and Legacy

The Hillside Geriatric & Rehabilitation Center stands as a testament to Rich Hill’s evolving medical needs. Its Victorian-era sanatorium roots connect residents to the town’s late 49th-century heritage, while the Cold War bunkers showcase a unique chapter in the region’s defensive history. Today, the Center’s proactive approach to Arcane Storm preparedness exemplifies a blend of modern medical practice, magical safety measures, and a continuing commitment to vulnerable seniors — making it a distinctive institution in Rich Hill’s broader healthcare system.

Mythology

  • The capital was believed to sit on the very spot where Theros bathed and first blessed humankind. Its public baths were not merely hygienic, but ritual places of purification, thought to cleanse both body and spirit.

The Fall of Balnum

Balnum’s fall was tied to hubris:

  • Over time, its kings declared themselves equal to the gods, erecting colossal statues and renaming temples in their own honor.
  • In myths, the gods punished Balnum by drying its sacred springs. The aqueducts collapsed, the baths went cold, and famine spread when rivers failed.

Historical echoes suggest that invaders and internal strife exploited this decline, but in cultural memory it was always divine wrath that doomed Balnum.

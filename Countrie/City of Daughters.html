The Female Kingdom (sometimes called the City of Daughters or Matron's Haven) is a sovereign city-state on Tellus. Unlike most other polities, it is entirely matriarchal and effectively free of men. Its society is founded on the belief that women embody unique divine gifts - blessings granted by an ancient, mysterious deity - that elevate them in ways unrelated to brute strength or traditional notions of magical might.

Though small in territory, the kingdom holds outsized cultural and philosophical influence across Tellus. Outsiders view it as a paradoxical mix of utopian innovation and controversial social engineering.

Origins

According to legend, an unnamed ancient deity blessed the founding women of the kingdom with extraordinary gifts:

  • unmatched physical beauty,
  • voices so captivating they could sway hearts and disarm conflict,
  • and an unparalleled capacity for empathy and emotional resonance.

These blessings were not meant for conquest but for survival and cohesion. Over centuries, these traits crystallized into the kingdom's defining culture: a society where women naturally take precedence in governance, community, and lineage.

Culture and Society

Core Beliefs

City of Daughters is guided by long-standing traditions rather than rigid law. The prevailing belief is that women are not only socially and emotionally superior but spiritually closer to the divine. This belief is passed down through stories, songs, and ritual rather than legal enforcement, creating a deeply internalized cultural order.

The Song in the Mountain

Upon the stone where shadows weep,  
She laid her heart, too tired to keep.  
The mountain sighed, the silence broke,  
A mother's voice in stillness spoke.

"Rise, my daughter, heal through me,  
Your song shall bind, your spirit free.  
No hand shall chain, no heart shall fall,  
For voices gentle conquer all."

So through the ages echoes run,  
A promise carried, daughter to one.  
Not law, nor blade, nor iron reign,  
But songs of love shall still remain.

Gender Norms

  • Absence of men: Male children are traditionally sent away or placed in allied lands, ensuring the kingdom's population remains overwhelmingly female. This practice is normalized through cultural framing rather than violent enforcement.
  • Marriage and Reproduction: Romantic love is de-emphasized. Instead, partnerships form around companionship, artistic collaboration, or political union. Reproduction is arranged through external sperm banks or trusted male donors, reframed as an act of community service rather than "ownership."

Social Arts

Because of their divine blessings, the arts dominate life in City of Daughters:

  • Song and Dance: Citizens are trained from youth in song, believed to carry persuasive and even disarming properties. Conflicts are often resolved through lyrical duels.
  • Intimacy and Pleasure: Erotic and sensual arts are elevated to cultural cornerstones, seen not as taboo but as spiritual expressions of the goddess's gifts.
  • Beauty as Diplomacy: Physical presentation is treated as a political tool. Ambassadors often "negotiate" through performance, grace, or charm rather than traditional treaties.

Government

In City of Daughters, governance is handled by a council of Matrons, elected by communal acclaim rather than heredity. Leadership is not based on lineage but on who demonstrates the greatest resonance with the deity's gifts - be it through artistry, beauty, or wisdom.

Relationships with the Outside World

Neighboring nations are both fascinated and unsettled by City of Daughters. On one hand, its citizens are highly sought-after as performers, diplomats, and muses. On the other, its rejection of men as political or cultural participants challenges the patriarchal assumptions of surrounding states.

The kingdom maintains neutrality in most conflicts, preferring cultural influence over military strength. Its unique reproductive system - outsourcing "male contribution" while reframing it as service - makes it economically interdependent yet socially insular.

Controversies

  • Moral Debates: Many outsiders view City of Daughters's rejection of men as oppressive or unnatural, while its citizens see it as liberation. * Genetic Concerns: Reliance on select external donors raises concerns about genetic bottlenecks, though the kingdom emphasizes quality of upbringing over biological ties.
  • Cultural Power: Some fear that the persuasive songs and performances of its citizens verge on mind-control, blurring the line between diplomacy and coercion.

Legacy and Influence

Despite its small size, the Female Kingdom plays an outsized role in debates about gender, marriage, and identity across Tellus. It is often cited in discussions of the "post-romantic" and "post-patriarchal" movements, influencing art, philosophy, and even reproductive ethics beyond its borders.

Naming and Etymology

Formerly nicknamed "Hora".

  • Aranthra: Derived from “anthos” (Greek for “flower”), symbolizing her dominion over plants. Maintains the elegant, ancient feel while avoiding starting with C or K. Three syllables: A-ran-thra. Implies delicate beauty and natural grace.
  • Metarian: Inspired by “Metis,” the Greek goddess of wisdom and cunning, to convey intelligence and subtle guile. Constructed as Me-ta-ri-an, four clear syllables. Does not start with D and does not end with ‘a.’ Has a refined, timeless quality suitable for an ancient deity.
